Brake System — Parts & Function

Click any numbered part in the diagram to see what it does and why it matters to the customer.

📍 By the end of this page you will be able to:
  • Name the four main brake components and what each one does
  • Explain why a customer can't self-diagnose brake wear by feel
  • Answer "my brakes feel fine" with a credible, specific response
📌
Why this matters for your calls: When a customer says "my brakes seem fine," you need to explain the difference between what they feel and what the technician measured.

👆 Click a numbered part in the diagram

Each number represents a key brake component. Click it to see what it does and why it wears out.

💿 Brake Rotor (Disc)

A flat, spinning metal disc attached to the wheel hub. When you press the brake pedal, the pads clamp against the rotor to create friction and stop the car.

💡 Rotors can reach 900°F in repeated hard braking. Even normal city driving regularly exceeds 300–400°F. Thin or grooved rotors can't handle that heat safely.

🔧 Brake Caliper

A hydraulic clamp that squeezes the brake pads against the rotor. It contains pistons pressurized by brake fluid. One caliper per wheel.

💡 A seized caliper piston keeps the pad constantly pressed against the rotor — causing rapid pad/rotor wear, heat buildup, and pulling to one side when braking.

🟡 Brake Pads

The friction material that contacts the rotor to slow the vehicle. Designed to wear down before the rotor. When the material is gone, metal hits metal.

💡 Pads have a wear indicator tab. At 2–3mm it scrapes the rotor — that squealing sound is your warning. Grinding means it's already too late and the rotor may be damaged.

💧 Brake Fluid (Hydraulic)

The incompressible fluid that transfers pressure from your brake pedal to the calipers. Brake fluid is hygroscopic — it absorbs moisture from the air over time.

💡 Water in brake fluid lowers its boiling point from ~450°F to as low as 280°F. In panic braking, it can vaporize — giving you a spongy pedal with zero stopping power.

Why It Wears Out

Every stop creates friction heat. Over time, the surface grooves, warps, and thins below safe limits. Measured in thickness — not visible to the naked eye.

🟡

Brake Pads

Why They Need Replacing

Pads are sacrificial — they're designed to wear down so the rotor doesn't. When the friction material is gone, metal hits metal. Grinding = expensive rotor damage.

🔧

Brake Caliper

What Goes Wrong

The caliper uses hydraulic pressure to squeeze the pads against the rotor. Pistons can seize or leak, causing uneven pad wear and pulling to one side when braking.

💧

The Hidden Danger

Brake fluid absorbs moisture over time. Water lowers its boiling point — under hard braking it can vaporize, creating a "spongy" pedal or complete brake failure.

✅ 8–12mm — New / Good Condition

Pad thickness is within safe limits. Normal friction material remains. No action needed at this time.

Metal-on-metal contact imminent or already occurring. Rotor damage likely. Safety risk. Replacement is not optional.

Engine Air Filter

What is it?

Function

Removes dust, pollen, and debris from air before it enters the engine. Clean air = proper fuel-to-air ratio = better performance & fuel economy.

Cabin Air Filter

What is it?

Function

Multi-layer filter (paper + activated carbon) that removes pollen, bacteria, exhaust fumes, and mold spores from the air that enters the passenger cabin.

What happens?

The Consequences

Restricted airflow forces the engine to work harder. Result: up to 10% worse fuel economy, rough idle, potential engine misfires, higher emissions.

What happens?

The Consequences

Reduced A/C efficiency, musty odors, mold growth on the evaporator, and passengers breathing unfiltered contaminants — especially risky for allergy sufferers.

Spark Plugs — The Ignition System

Understand the combustion cycle to sell this service with confidence.

📍 By the end of this lesson you will be able to:
  • Explain the combustion cycle and where spark plugs fit in it
  • Describe what electrode gap wear causes in plain customer language
  • Sell iridium/platinum upgrades using the gap-maintenance benefit
📌
A spark plug fires thousands of times per minute. When the electrode wears down, the gap widens, the spark weakens, and combustion becomes inconsistent. That's a misfire — and the customer is losing money every mile they drive it.

⚡ Combustion Cycle — Animated

💨
1 — INTAKE

Intake valve opens. Piston moves down, drawing in a mix of air and fuel.

🔄
2 — COMPRESSION

Both valves close. Piston moves up, compressing the mix to a fraction of its original volume.

⚡
3 — POWER

Spark plug fires. The compressed mix ignites, forcing the piston down and turning the crankshaft.

💨
4 — EXHAUST

Exhaust valve opens. Piston pushes burned gases out. Cycle repeats thousands of times per minute.

Why it matters for your sell: A worn spark plug misfires during Stage 3. Each misfire = unburned fuel wasted, power lost, and emissions increased. The customer doesn't feel it happen — but they do pay for it at the pump.

🔍 Spark Plug Anatomy — Click a Part to Learn

👆 Click any part of the plug

Each section of the spark plug has a specific job. Click directly on the diagram or use the buttons above to explore each part.

🔩 A — Hex Nut / Terminal

The top of the spark plug where the ignition coil wire connects. The hex nut allows it to be tightened into the cylinder head with a socket wrench.

💡 Torque spec matters — over-tightened plugs can strip the cylinder head threads ($500+ repair). Under-tightened plugs can back out under compression pressure.

⬡ B — Ceramic Insulator

The white porcelain center section that insulates the high-voltage center electrode from the metal shell, preventing the charge from shorting before reaching the tip.

💡 The ignition coil sends 12,000–45,000 volts through the plug. The ceramic insulator handles that voltage on every single firing cycle.

⬛ C — Steel Shell / Threads

The outer metal casing that screws into the cylinder head and grounds the plug to the engine block. Contains the ground electrode at the tip.

💡 The shell absorbs and dissipates heat from combustion. Wrong heat range plug = fouling or pre-ignition — both cause misfires and engine damage.

⭐ D — Center & Ground Electrodes / Gap

The center electrode carries the high-voltage charge. The gap between it and the ground electrode is where the spark jumps — must be precise (0.6–1.2mm).

💡 As electrodes wear, the gap grows. Wider gap = weaker spark = incomplete combustion = misfires and fuel waste. Iridium/platinum plugs maintain gap longer.
💡
Talk Track Tip: When a customer asks why spark plugs cost so much, explain that modern iridium and platinum plugs need to fire millions of times across their lifespan — the material cost reflects that precision engineering.
⚡

Worn Spark Plugs

What do you feel?

Symptoms

Rough idle, engine misfire (shaking), slower acceleration, harder cold starts, drop in fuel economy. Customer notices "the car doesn't feel right."

🔍

The Electrode Gap

Why it matters

What Happens

New plugs have a precise gap (0.6–1.2mm). As they wear, the gap widens. A bigger gap = weaker spark = incomplete combustion = waste of fuel and increased emissions.

🔢

4-Cyl vs 6-Cyl

Why the price difference?

The Math

4-cylinder = 4 plugs. 6-cylinder = 6 plugs + often harder to access (rear bank buried in the engine bay). More parts + more labor = higher price. Completely fair.

🚨

Ignoring Worn Plugs

What's the risk?

Escalation Risk

Misfires can damage the catalytic converter ($800–$2,000+) and ignition coils ($200+ each). A $395–$805 spark plug service can prevent $2,000+ in downstream repairs.

30K–100KMiles between plug changes (varies by type)
0.6–1.2mmCorrect electrode gap — precision engineering
$2,000+Catalytic converter damage from ignored misfires
3%–30%Fuel economy loss from worn spark plugs
🎙️ Auditory — Say This Out Loud
"Your spark plugs fire thousands of times a minute. As the electrodes wear down, the gap gets wider and the spark gets weaker — that's a misfire. You might not feel it yet, but your fuel economy is already dropping and your engine is working harder than it needs to."
⚡ Quick Check

What is the customer-facing benefit of iridium spark plugs over standard copper?

✅ That's the sell. Longer gap maintenance = fewer misfires = sustained fuel economy.
❌ Partially true physics but not the primary selling point. Gap maintenance and longevity is your message.
❌ Not accurate. Hyundai specifies OEM plugs — iridium is a premium upgrade, not a requirement.
⭐ Key Takeaway — Lesson 3
  • Worn plugs cause misfires customers don't feel — but they feel the higher gas bill
  • The electrode gap is your conversation anchor: it widens, spark weakens, combustion suffers
  • Iridium/platinum = longer interval between services — worth the upgrade price

Fluid Services — Why Fluids Fail

All fluids degrade. When they do, the parts they protect fail next.

📍 By the end of this lesson you will be able to:
  • Explain how and why fluids degrade over time and mileage
  • Connect fluid condition to component protection using plain language
  • Explain why fluid condition matters more than fluid level

💧 Fluid Degradation Simulator — Drag to See What Happens

Select a condition to see how fluid degrades over time:

✅ New — Clean & Clear

Full viscosity, no contamination. Proper lubrication and heat protection. No action needed.

⚠️ Aging — Dark Brown

Oxidation building. Additive package depleting. Losing protective properties. Recommend service at next visit.

🚨 Degraded — Near-Black / Burnt

Heavy oxidation, metal particles, moisture. No longer protecting components. Accelerates component wear.

"The tech found your fluid has broken down — it's black and contaminated. Leaving it in will wear out the transmission. A fluid service flushes out the degraded fluid and replaces it with fresh, protected fluid."

Power steering pump, rack and pinion, and steering lines. Worn fluid causes pump whine, heavy steering, and seal leaks. Power steering replacement: $500–$1,500.

Evaporator core, A/C ducts, and drain lines. Mold and bacteria grow on the evaporator, causing musty smell and reduced airflow. BG treatment eliminates it and restores A/C efficiency.

"Fluids don't last forever — they break down from heat and use. When the additive package is gone, the fluid can't protect the parts anymore. fluid services flush out degraded fluid and replace it — preventing the kind of component failures that cost thousands."
⚡ Quick Check

A customer asks: "My fluid looks fine — it's not leaking and the level is full." How do you respond?

✅ Strong answer. The additive package depletes invisibly. This is the key education moment.
❌ Never undermine the tech. Full level ≠ good condition. The chemical protection degrades regardless.
❌ Interval-only answers don't persuade. Explain the "why" — chemical degradation is your strongest argument.
⭐ Key Takeaway — Lesson 4
  • "Full and not leaking" ≠ good condition — the additive package depletes with heat and use
  • Dark, contaminated fluid means the parts it's supposed to protect are already under stress
  • Fluid degradation is invisible — the customer won't feel it until a component has already started failing

📋 Key Takeaways

  • myKaarma puts the customer at the center — use universal search first, every time. Stop hunting through screens.
  • The History tab, MPI view, and Declined Items panel replace 3–4 separate CDK screens. One tab. Sixty seconds. Full picture.
  • Every confirmation in myKaarma is automatic (SMS + email + DMS push). Verbally bridge the close while it sends.
  • Hyundai recall data and factory menus populate live from the VIN — always update mileage at the start of a call to trigger the menu.
  • Lead recall conversations with "no-charge, covered by GM." Lead capacity conversations with two time options, not open-ended questions.
  • Red capacity = do not book without SA approval. Yellow capacity = create urgency, convert fast.

✅ Your Action Items This Week

  • Complete at least 10 supervised practice bookings in myKaarma before going solo on live calls.
  • Memorize the three shortcuts: Ctrl+K (search), Ctrl+N (new appointment), Ctrl+M (message thread).
  • On your next 5 inbound calls, pull the customer profile before saying anything past your greeting.
  • Complete the free Scheduler+ Course at mykaarma.com/scheduler-course — takes 45 minutes.
  • Practice Talk Track 1 and 2 with a colleague using real profiles in training mode before going live.
